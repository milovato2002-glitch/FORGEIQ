Your Supplement Stack
Tier 1 — Always Recommended
⚛
Protein Powder
20-40g post-workout within 45 min
Whey, casein, or plant-based. Foundation of muscle recovery and satiety.
⚛
Creatine Monohydrate
3-5g daily
Most researched supplement in sports nutrition. Supports strength, power, and cognitive function.
⚛
Vitamin D3 + K2
1000-2000 IU D3 + 100-200mcg K2
Bone health, immune function, mood. K2 directs calcium to bones, not arteries.
⚛
Magnesium Glycinate
310-420mg before bed
Muscle recovery, sleep quality, stress management. Most people are deficient.
⚛
Omega-3 Fish Oil
2-3g EPA+DHA daily
Anti-inflammatory, heart health, brain function, joint support.
Tier 2 — Population-Specific
◆
Collagen Peptides + Vitamin C
10-15g collagen + 500mg Vitamin C
Skin elasticity, joint repair, connective tissue. Essential for post-weight-loss and 50+ athletes.
◆
HMB (Beta-Hydroxy Beta-Methylbutyrate)
3g daily in divided doses
Reduces muscle breakdown during caloric deficit. Critical for GLP-1 users and new lifters.
◆
Fiber / Psyllium Husk
5-10g daily, gradually increase
GLP-1 mandatory. Supports digestion and gut health during appetite suppression.
◆
Electrolytes
Training days minimum; GLP-1: daily mandatory
Sodium, potassium, magnesium. Prevents cramping, fatigue, brain fog.
◆
Ashwagandha KSM-66
300-600mg daily
Cortisol reduction, stress resilience, sleep quality. Studied extensively for anxiety and PTSD.
◆
Zinc + Copper
15-30mg Zinc + 1-2mg Copper (always paired)
Immune function, testosterone support, wound healing. Always take copper with zinc to prevent depletion.
◆
B-Complex with B12
1 daily with food
GLP-1 high priority. Energy metabolism, nervous system support. Reduced absorption common with GLP-1.
Tier 3 — Educational Only
●
Beta-Alanine
May improve endurance in 1-4 minute high-intensity efforts. Tingling side effect common.
●
BCAAs
Branched-chain amino acids. Largely unnecessary if protein intake is adequate.
●
Glutamine
Gut health support. Minimal evidence for muscle building in well-nourished individuals.
●
CLA (Conjugated Linoleic Acid)
Modest fat loss effects in some studies. Results are small and inconsistent.
●
Fat Burners
Thermogenics with caffeine and stimulants. Risk often outweighs minimal benefit.
●
Testosterone Boosters
Most OTC products have no significant effect. Consult endocrinologist for actual concerns.
●
Peptides
BPC-157, TB-500, etc. Research is emerging. Regulatory gray area. Medical supervision required.
Educational only — not recommended. Included for awareness so you can make informed decisions with your physician.
