SEMAGLUTIDE

Ozempic Wegovy Rybelsus

Mechanism of Action

GLP-1 receptor agonist with 94% structural similarity to human GLP-1. Binds to GLP-1 receptors in the pancreas, brain, and gut. Slows gastric emptying, reduces appetite, and improves insulin sensitivity.

FDA Approval

  • Ozempic — 2017 (Type 2 Diabetes)
  • Wegovy — 2021 (Chronic weight management)
  • Rybelsus — 2019 (Oral formulation for Type 2 Diabetes)

Clinical Trial Results

STEP trials — average 14.9% body weight reduction over 68 weeks (Wegovy). STEP 1: 86.4% of participants achieved at least 5% weight loss.

Muscle Loss Risk

25-39% of weight lost can be lean mass without resistance training (Frontiers in Endocrinology 2026). This is a critical consideration for anyone on semaglutide therapy.

Side Effects

  • Nausea (44%)
  • Diarrhea (30%)
  • Vomiting (24%)
  • Constipation (24%)
  • Gastroparesis risk
  • Thyroid C-cell tumors (animal studies)
  • Pancreatitis (rare)

Fitness Protocol Notes

Mandatory resistance training 3x/week minimum. Protein intake 1.2-1.6g/kg bodyweight. Creatine 3-5g daily. Electrolytes mandatory.

Wilding JPH et al. "Once-Weekly Semaglutide in Adults with Overweight or Obesity." New England Journal of Medicine, 2021.

TIRZEPATIDE

Mounjaro Zepbound

Mechanism of Action

Dual GIP + GLP-1 receptor agonist (first of its kind). Activates both glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide and GLP-1 receptors simultaneously.

FDA Approval

  • Mounjaro — 2022 (Type 2 Diabetes)
  • Zepbound — 2023 (Chronic weight management)

Clinical Trial Results

SURMOUNT-1 — average 20.9% body weight reduction at 72 weeks (15mg dose). 36% achieved at least 25% weight loss. Superior to semaglutide in head-to-head comparisons.

Muscle Loss Risk

Same as semaglutide without resistance training. Lean mass preservation requires dedicated strength programming.

Side Effects

  • Nausea (29%)
  • Diarrhea (23%)
  • Vomiting (12%)
  • Constipation (12%)
  • Injection site reactions

Fitness Protocol Notes

Same as semaglutide — resistance training is critical for preserving lean mass during treatment.

Jastreboff AM et al. "Tirzepatide Once Weekly for the Treatment of Obesity." New England Journal of Medicine, 2022.
📚 PubMed 🔬 ClinicalTrials

LIRAGLUTIDE

Victoza Saxenda

Mechanism of Action

GLP-1 receptor agonist with 97% structural similarity to human GLP-1. Daily injection (versus weekly for semaglutide).

FDA Approval

  • Victoza — 2010 (Type 2 Diabetes)
  • Saxenda — 2014 (Chronic weight management)

Clinical Trial Results

SCALE trials — average 8% body weight reduction over 56 weeks. Earlier generation with less weight loss than semaglutide.

Side Effects

  • Nausea (40%)
  • Diarrhea (21%)
  • Constipation (19%)
  • Vomiting (16%)
  • Headache (14%)
Pi-Sunyer X et al. "A Randomized, Controlled Trial of 3.0 mg of Liraglutide in Weight Management." New England Journal of Medicine, 2015.

DULAGLUTIDE

Trulicity

Mechanism of Action

GLP-1 receptor agonist. Weekly injection. Primarily for Type 2 Diabetes with moderate weight loss effects.

FDA Approval

  • Trulicity — 2014 (Type 2 Diabetes)

Clinical Trial Results

REWIND trial — modest weight loss (approximately 3-4 kg over 5 years), with primary cardiovascular outcome benefit.

📚 PubMed

EXENATIDE

Byetta (twice daily) Bydureon (weekly)

Mechanism of Action

Synthetic exendin-4, a GLP-1 receptor agonist derived from Gila monster venom. The first GLP-1 receptor agonist approved.

FDA Approval

  • Byetta — 2005
  • Bydureon — 2012

Clinical Trial Results

Moderate weight loss (2-4 kg). First generation agent, largely replaced by newer medications.

📚 PubMed

ORAL SEMAGLUTIDE

Rybelsus

Mechanism of Action

Same active compound as injectable semaglutide but formulated with SNAC (sodium N-[8-(2-hydroxybenzoyl)amino] caprylate) absorption enhancer for oral bioavailability. Must be taken fasting with 4 oz of water or less, 30 minutes before food.

FDA Approval

  • Rybelsus — 2019 (Type 2 Diabetes), weight management indication in trials

Clinical Trial Results

PIONEER trials — weight loss less than injectable formulation but still significant (approximately 5-7%).

📚 PubMed

GLP-2 RECEPTOR AGONISTS

GLP-2 is a completely different pathway from GLP-1. These medications are NOT used for weight loss. Included here for educational completeness.

TEDUGLUTIDE

Gattex (US) Revestive (EU)

Mechanism of Action

GLP-2 receptor agonist. NOT for weight loss. Promotes intestinal mucosal growth, increases villus height, crypt depth, and mesenteric blood flow.

FDA Approval

  • Gattex — 2012 (Short Bowel Syndrome / SBS)

Clinical Context

GLP-2 is a completely different pathway from GLP-1. While GLP-1 agonists target appetite and glucose metabolism, GLP-2 agonists target intestinal health and nutrient absorption. Teduglutide is used exclusively for patients with Short Bowel Syndrome who are dependent on parenteral nutrition.

ORAL GLP THERAPY

The shift from injectable to oral delivery represents one of the most significant advances in GLP medication development.

ORAL SEMAGLUTIDE (RYBELSUS)

Rybelsus FDA Approved

Oral Delivery Mechanism

Rybelsus uses SNAC (sodium N-[8-(2-hydroxybenzoyl)amino] caprylate) as an absorption enhancer. SNAC creates a localized pH increase in the stomach, protecting semaglutide from degradation and facilitating transcellular absorption through the gastric epithelium.

Dosing Requirements

  • Must be taken on an empty stomach
  • Swallow whole with no more than 4 oz (120 mL) of plain water
  • Wait at least 30 minutes before eating, drinking, or taking other oral medications
  • Available in 3 mg, 7 mg, and 14 mg tablets

Limitations vs Injectable

  • Lower bioavailability than subcutaneous injection (approximately 1% oral bioavailability)
  • Less weight loss than injectable semaglutide (approximately 5-7% vs 14.9%)
  • Strict fasting requirements reduce convenience
  • Food and other medications significantly reduce absorption

Clinical Trial Results

PIONEER trials demonstrated significant weight loss, though less than injectable formulations. PIONEER 1-10 trials established efficacy across multiple populations.

ORFORGLIPRON — THE FUTURE OF ORAL GLP

Phase 3 Clinical Trials

Why Orforglipron Changes Everything

Unlike Rybelsus, orforglipron is a small molecule — not a peptide. This means it does not require the complex SNAC absorption enhancer system and eliminates the strict fasting requirements that limit Rybelsus.

Advantages Over Rybelsus

  • No fasting requirement before dosing
  • No injection needed
  • Small molecule — potentially lower manufacturing cost
  • Better oral bioavailability as a non-peptide compound
  • Room-temperature stable (no cold chain requirement)

Early Results

Phase 2 — up to 14.7% weight loss at 36 weeks, approaching injectable semaglutide efficacy with oral convenience.

Developer

Eli Lilly and Company

TRAINING ON GLP MEDICATIONS — THE PROTOCOL

Evidence-based training and nutrition protocols designed to preserve lean mass and optimize body composition during GLP medication use. Developed by Dr. Lovato, NASM CNC/CES/PES.

1

RESISTANCE TRAINING

Minimum 3x/week, targeting all major muscle groups. Compound movements prioritized: squat, deadlift, bench press, overhead press, rows. Progressive overload tracked session-to-session. Phase-appropriate rep ranges: hypertrophy (8-12 reps), strength (4-6 reps), endurance (12-15 reps). Without structured resistance training, 25-39% of weight lost on GLP medications will be lean mass.

Frontiers in Endocrinology, 2026 — Lean mass loss during GLP-1 RA therapy without resistance exercise.
2

PROTEIN INTAKE

1.2-1.6g/kg bodyweight daily — prioritize protein at every meal. Use the "protein first" eating strategy: consume protein before carbohydrates and fats at each meal. Target a leucine threshold of 2.5g per meal to maximize muscle protein synthesis. GLP medications reduce appetite, making protein prioritization critical. Consider protein supplementation (whey, casein, plant-based) if whole food intake is insufficient.

Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition, 2025 — Protein requirements during pharmacological weight loss.
3

INJECTION DAY MANAGEMENT

Reduce workout intensity by 20-30% on injection day. Nausea management: consume a light, protein-rich meal 1-2 hours before training. Avoid high-intensity training on injection day. Schedule injections on rest days or light training days when possible. Stay hydrated — nausea is often exacerbated by dehydration. Ginger tea or ginger supplements may help manage GI side effects.

4

PROGRESSIVE OVERLOAD

Track every session. Volume (sets x reps x weight) must increase over time to preserve and build muscle during caloric deficit. Use a training log or app. Even small increments (2.5 lb increases, 1 additional rep) constitute progressive overload. During GLP-medicated caloric deficit, maintaining strength is the primary goal — any strength gain is exceptional.

5

CREATINE MONOHYDRATE

3-5g daily creatine monohydrate. The supplement with the strongest evidence base for muscle preservation during GLP medication use. Improves intracellular hydration, enhances ATP regeneration, and supports lean mass retention during caloric deficit. No loading phase necessary — consistent daily dosing achieves saturation within 3-4 weeks. Take with any meal.

Frontiers in Nutrition, 2025 — Creatine supplementation during pharmacological caloric restriction.
6

HMB (BETA-HYDROXY BETA-METHYLBUTYRATE)

3g daily HMB. Anti-catabolic supplement that helps prevent muscle protein breakdown during caloric deficit. Particularly effective in the early weeks of GLP therapy when caloric restriction is most aggressive. Can be taken with meals — split into 1g three times daily for optimal blood levels. Works synergistically with resistance training and adequate protein.

Frontiers in Nutrition, 2025 — HMB and muscle preservation during medically-supervised weight loss.
7

HYDRATION AND ELECTROLYTES

Minimum 3L water daily. Electrolytes are mandatory — not optional. GLP medications can cause significant fluid loss through GI side effects (nausea, diarrhea, vomiting). Key electrolytes: sodium (2000-3000mg), potassium (3500-4700mg from food and supplements), magnesium (400-600mg). Consider an electrolyte supplement without added sugar. Signs of electrolyte imbalance: muscle cramps, dizziness, heart palpitations, fatigue.

8

SLEEP OPTIMIZATION

7-9 hours nightly. GLP medications can affect sleep quality — some users report difficulty falling or staying asleep. Magnesium glycinate (200-400mg) before bed supports both sleep quality and electrolyte balance. Maintain consistent sleep/wake times. Avoid screens 1 hour before bed. Keep the bedroom cool (65-68F). Poor sleep increases cortisol, which promotes lean mass loss during caloric deficit.

GLP MEDICATIONS AND YOUR SKIN — DEBORA'S GUIDE

By Debora Lovato, Licensed Esthetician, NASM CNC/CES/PES, GLP-1 Skin Specialist

FACIAL VOLUME LOSS

Mechanism: Rapid fat loss reduces buccal and malar fat pads — the fat compartments that give your face its youthful volume and contour. This is sometimes called "Ozempic face" in popular media, though it occurs with any rapid weight loss method.

Timeline: Typically noticeable 3-6 months into treatment, especially with faster weight loss rates.

Management

  • Hyaluronic acid dermal fillers (consult a board-certified dermatologist or plastic surgeon)
  • Facial exercises to maintain muscle tone in the face
  • Collagen supplementation (15g collagen peptides daily)
  • Gradual weight loss rate — discuss titration speed with your physician
  • Topical retinoids to support skin cell turnover

BODY SKIN LAXITY

Why it happens: Skin collagen and elastin fibers cannot retract fast enough to keep pace with rapid weight loss. The skin's structural proteins need time to remodel. Age, sun exposure history, smoking history, and genetics all affect skin elasticity.

Prevention

  • Slower weight loss rate (1-2 lbs/week maximum when possible)
  • Collagen supplementation (15g daily with Vitamin C for absorption)
  • Adequate hydration (minimum 3L daily)
  • Resistance training to fill the space under the skin with muscle

Treatment Options

  • RF (radiofrequency) skin tightening — non-invasive
  • Microneedling — stimulates collagen production
  • Laser treatments — fractional CO2, Erbium
  • Surgical options — abdominoplasty, arm lift, thigh lift (for severe cases)

HAIR CHANGES

Telogen effluvium: Stress-related hair shedding triggered by rapid weight loss. The body redirects resources away from hair growth during significant caloric deficit. This is a physiological response, not a direct medication side effect.

Timeline: Typically begins 2-4 months after significant weight loss. Usually resolves 6-12 months after weight stabilizes, assuming adequate nutrition.

Intervention

  • Biotin supplementation (5000 mcg daily)
  • Zinc (15-30 mg daily)
  • Check iron levels — ferritin should be above 40 ng/mL
  • Protein adequacy is critical — inadequate protein worsens hair loss
  • Consider a dermatologist referral if shedding is severe or prolonged

DEBORA'S 5-STEP POST-GLP SKIN PROTOCOL

A comprehensive approach to skin health during and after GLP medication therapy.

Step 1

Collagen + Vitamin C
Collagen peptides 15g/day + 50mg Vitamin C, taken 30-60 minutes before training. Vitamin C is essential for collagen synthesis. Studies show that collagen peptide supplementation combined with resistance training improves skin elasticity and hydration.

Step 2

Omega-3 Fatty Acids
2-3g EPA+DHA daily from fish oil or algae-based supplement. Omega-3s support skin barrier function, reduce inflammation, and improve skin hydration from the inside out. Take with a fat-containing meal for optimal absorption.

Step 3

Hydration Protocol
Minimum 3L water daily + oral hyaluronic acid supplement (120-240mg daily). Hyaluronic acid holds up to 1000x its weight in water. Combined with adequate water intake, this supports skin plumpness and elasticity from within.

Step 4

Professional Treatments
Monthly facials with a licensed esthetician. Quarterly RF (radiofrequency) skin tightening or microneedling sessions. These professional treatments stimulate collagen and elastin production at deeper skin layers that topical products cannot reach.

Step 5

Daily Protection
SPF 30+ every single day — even indoors, even on cloudy days. UV damage is the number one cause of collagen breakdown. Use medical-grade skincare: retinol (evening), Vitamin C serum (morning), and a quality moisturizer with ceramides.
